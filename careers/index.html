Hiring: Senior Researcher


Job Description

We are looking for a senior (8 to 12 years experience) researcher to lead in depth research at the intersection of media, development, and technology. Through the research you do as part of your job, we expect to find answers to fundamental questions like:
  • How is the Indian news media ecosystem covering development issues like Gender, Health, Inclusion, etc? How do editors decide which stories to cover?
  • What is the expanse of the policy making system in India? Who are the actors in it? What pieces of information do they generate? In which step of the policy making process do they participate in?
  • How do development organisations use digital media to engage various stakeholders?
  • How do journalists and journalism organisations use technology?
  • What are the skilling needs in the civic media community?

To do such research, you will need to help us define the problem statement, do literature study if others have attempted to answer questions like this, define the methodology of how to collect data, conduct surveys or analyze publicly available data, draw insights and finally write the report.

The location of this job is in New Delhi.

Requirements

  • 8-10 years of experience with research (qualitative and quantitative), preferably on themes related to the media, communication, development, and inclusion
  • Field research experience - familiarity with interviewing and surveying techniques
  • A flair for documentation and detailed record keeping
  • Awareness of/experience working in the Indian news industry would be an advantage
  • Experience leading teams in Indian newsrooms; portfolio of compelling digital-led storytelling;
  • Ability to develop project case studies (with compelling copy, documentation, presentations and budgets) that showcase results, maximize impact and help attract additional partners and talent;
  • Indian citizenship or Indian work visa.

Preferred

  • Exceptional public, interpersonal, written communication skills in English and any Indian language;
  • Know-how to collaborate with developers and designers and work effectively in a team;
  • Advanced experience with work tools like Slack and project management tools like GetFlow as well as slides and spreadsheets;
  • Ability to engage with and secure buy-in from senior-most management, other key stakeholders
  • Previous experience as a journalist; reputation as an emerging media leader; visible participation in communities of journalists and digital professionals;
  • Ability to manage several concurrent projects with attention to detail and respect for deadlines;
  • Ability to prioritize and solve problems with limited direction, but when required to, take direction without bias against age, level of experience or socioeconomic background;

How to apply

Sounds like the right gig for you? Send us an email at Nasr ul Hadi or Ritvvij Parrikh with a compelling case with clear evidence of your skills, experience, contributions to key projects and their achieved outcomes. Is any of your research public? If yes, share the reports with us.

About us

PROTO is an antidisciplinary research and community business in India. Our clients — such as the International Center for Journalists — are invested in the future of digital media, as a driver of impact around specific civic issues. We enable this with:

  • Research that develops experimental models, strategy, technology for better storytelling
  • Community that serves as a peer-to-peer network for upskilling, resources, opportunities
